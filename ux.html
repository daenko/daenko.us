UX redesign

In 2014 before my studies at De Anza I did research on usability of university websites. I have analyzed and compared some of American and Belarusian universities' sites. I have published several articles with practical recommendations on how to change university's site layout and content intended to enhance user experience and to attain more foreign students. I have reviewed our current site bsuir.by (Belarusian State University of Informatics and Radioelectronics, Minsk, Belarus) and created the redesign checklist. A short summary of my article in Russian (pp.21-22) could be found here. Below are some of my recommendations.

Navigation and textual content of the site

When entering the site it is immediately obvious that left navigation is too small, so I suggested to enlarge it. The other problem is the main content contaning too much text, organized into paragraphs which are the only elements on the page. Besides this all of the text is aligned flush left, I suggested to separate content under headings, or items in lists, and to add more buttons, images, links, tabular data.

Homepage

The important part of redesign is homepage and I recommended to remove Why BSUIR section from the homepage to a separate page, and to serve homepage as the main access point for site's message, branding, and navigation. I also recommended to redesign Why BSUIR page to make any written content on it as simple and concise as possible.

New pages and navigation titles

I recommended to add new pages such as Majors and Degrees, Alumni. These pages contain specific terms and headings that foreign students got used to. I also recommended to use specific terms in navigation titles.

News and events

One of my recommendations was to add News and Events pages for each department, university's club, workshops. These pages are necessary to present important upcoming events from scientific conferences to students fieldtrips. These pages are intended not only to show university's life but to involve students to university's activities, to enable them to participate. Unfortunately English version of a website doesn't contain a lot of News and Events pages, but News page has been developed for Research Department.

News page

While not every recommendation has been considered during redesign process, but as a result BSUIR's website has become more modern, user-friendly, and good looking. There are many more things to improve, for example:

  • add a calendar of events
  • add mobile-friendly responsive design
  • make webpages dynamic and interactive with forms enchancing students' participation
  • display more multimedia: movies, animations and videos.
