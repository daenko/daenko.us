Idioms are word combinations which have a different meaning than the literal meanings of each word. For example, raining cats and dogs means raining heavily. I forgot my umbrella, and it was raining cats and dogs.. Horse around means to play roughly. Schoolkids laughing and horsing around.

Practising idioms

Idiom Meaning and example Our examples
source: https://www.englishclub.com/vocabulary/idioms-animal.htm

ants in one's pants 		unable to sit still or remain calm out of nervousness or excitement Lisa had ants in her pants the day before her interview.

copy cat 		a person who does the same thing as someone else My sister is such a copy cat. First she bought the same car as me, and now she's applying to my school.

let the cat out of the bag 		reveal a secret carelessly or by mistake Who let the cat out of the bag about the surprise party?

have a cow 		get extremely upset My teacher had a cow when she realized nobody had done the homework.

rat race 		fierce, competitive struggle for power, position etc I'm ready to leave this rat race and retire in Mexico.

smell a rat 		begin to suspect trickery etc I asked my brothers not to tell my parents that I went out, but I could smell a rat as soon as I opened the door.
