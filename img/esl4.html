Propaganda techniques

In the essay "Propaganda techniques in Today's Advertising" McClintock argues that Americans are being seduced and brainwashed, often willingly, by advertisors and politicians. He maintains that propaganda is an effort to influence people's behavior and opinions. It's important to be aware of main techniques and to preserve the independence of thoughts and behavior. Why does propaganda work? Because it appeals our emotions, not mind, it capitalize on our prejudices and laziness because it does our thinking for us.

Name calling

Make audience feel mistrustful, fearful, or angry. Negative feelings.

Glittering generalities

Surrounding of products with attractive labels, words, phrases. "Vote for the future!"

Transfer

Improve the image of a product by associating it with symbols most people respect. "Political ads, of course, practically wrap themselves in the flag."

Testimonial

Capitalizes on admiration people have to celebrity.

Card stacking

The suppression or distortion of information. One side may tell "half truth", suppress evidence. "Sticker prise $1,000 lower than most comparable cars".

Plain folks

The aim to persuade us that some person or company "is just like you", "has the same values".

"Were it left to me to decide whether we should have a government without newspapers or newspapers without government, I should not hesitate a moment to prefer a latter."

Thomas Jefferson third president od United sates

Bandwagon

An appeal to people's desire to do what others are doing. "Everyone is doing it. Why don't you?"
