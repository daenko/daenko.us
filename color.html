3. Color theory in Web Design

Why are colors of website important? How do they influence users and usability? What message do they carry? How to choose a pleasing color scheme? How to combine colors? Let's try to answer these questions.

Emotional approach

How do the colors on the site affect user's mood and emotional expression? It is important to recognize how colors influence our emotions and creates harmony with site's idea. Each color could be associated with some symbol as it is shown on the following images.

The examples of colors symbols

There are usually one primary, one secondary, and highlight color on the site, as we can read in the Combining color article. Let's take a look at combination of colors to gain correct emotional view. Simple example, if the site is about parks and recreation, you won't use red and orange colors but some colors of nature: green and blue. These green and blue colors may not work for site with kid's games because of being too boring for kids. Blue and white are clean and fresh, so it is good idea to use them for site of health theme. Brown and yellow works for cooking site, orange and black could be used for sport, etc.

Temperature of the site

Is your site supposed to be warm, sunny, and joyful? Then the warm color from red to yellow should be primary on the site. If you want clean and fresh feelings from the site, use blue colors from light to dark. In this case cold colors are dominated, and green color tends to be cold.

Green color could be considered as warm or as cold depending on combination

Looking at these pictures you may notice that cold colors appear to be more distant, while warmer colors appear to be closer. That is why it is a good idea to use a warm color for a menu, and to use the cold colors for backgrounds, as Combining colors article says. Could we combine warm and cold colors for site theme? Yes, but then the discussion turns to the color contrast types.

Color contrast

The primary colors consist of three hues (red, yellow, blue) from which we can mix all other hues. Contrast in hue means high contrast because the colors are situated in the opposite sides of the color wheel. If you want to highlight some object, high contrast is the good idea. It is also good idea to make high contrast between text and its background.

Examples of high contrast

In order to make contrast not so strong, use contrast in tint and shade. Tint of the color means that white color is added to that color, while shade means that the color becomes more darker because of the black color adding.

Less color contrast: in tint (left) and in shade

The other term of color theory is saturation, so it means how different is the color from the pure grey, which makes color intensity. Contrast in saturation is shown on the following picture and could vary from high to low. If you use just one single hue and its variations of tints, shades and saturation, you'll get monotone color scheme. Some designers suggests to avoid fully monochromatic scheme. But using saturation and tint/shade variations of a color is good idea and can be efficient in background, but bad for the text. Try to avoid low contrast in text and its background. It decreases usability of your site and makes a lot of problems for people with disabilities to read your content.

Color contrast: monotone site is not good, low contrast between text and background is bad

Color harmony

While color harmony could be subjectible, color theory offers some guidelines to combine colors on the website. These guidelines inlude:

  • choose colors that meet the idea of the site
  • make sure that site has desired temperature
  • make sure that combinations of the colors are not too strong or too low
  • find good combination for primary and secondary colors
  • make text highly contrasted.
