Openness as Catalyst for an Educational Reformation

I've learned from the article Openness as Catalyst for an Educational Reformation what does openness of education mean. It means that educational recourses are provided for students for free under a copyright license and permission to reuse, revise, remix, and redistribute them. The author tells us that "If a teacher is not sharing what he or she knows, there is no education happening", so he equals sharing and education, and underlines the role of Internet in this process.

The author gives great example from the history about the printing press technology and efforts of church to monopolize access to information. He compared it to nowadays when access to educational resources is closed and the prime aim of educators is commercializing. He believes that education is developing and evolves toward openness and information technologies "have a critical role to play in the future of education".

Improving user experiences through feedback and iteration - What does iteration mean? I found the definition of iterative design in wikipedia, so as far as I understand it means testing and consists of some steps. The first one is prototype developing, then it is evaluated by focus group, the information from this group is incorporated into the next iteration of design. Because changes are easiest and less expensive to implement in the earliest stages of development, this approach makes sense.
