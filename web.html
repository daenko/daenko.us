This is summary of the article Writing for the Web

The main idea is to show how web content could be improved from the usability point of view. The most interesting is that the author gives the example HOW to do it. The author starts from the fact that people do not read Web pages word by word, but they scan it, picking out individual words and sentences. He underlined that credibility suffers when users clearly see that the site exaggerates.

The author shows concrete steps to improve some piece of text so called "promotional writing". The first step is making this text concise. The second step relates to scannable layout that is more user friendly. Then the author edits style of the text using more neutral language, he calls it objective language. Last, the author combines these 3 writing styles together: concise, scannable, and objective and gives the final version of the text. He shows that the usability improvement is 124% relative to the first text with promotional style.

Accessibility simulations

Accessibility is about making your website accessible to all Internet users—everyone who wants to view it, both disabled and abled. Read How to Make Your Website More Accessible. Describe your experience in 2-3 sentences on the Dyslexia Simulations

I tried WebAIM Dyslexia Simulation, now I know about this disability and how simple acceptability techniques could help these people. These techniques are: keeping navigational schemes as consistent as possible, smaller paragraphs, providing illustrations, printable page version, using proportional sizing. It costs developers nothing, and it also helps users without disabilities.
