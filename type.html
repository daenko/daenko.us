6. Typography

What is good typography for web pages? What are the elements of good typography? I tried to look more closely at these questions and to find good examples. I didn't realize earlier how important to use really good typography for a web site.

"Character" of the site

Content is the most important part of a website, and typography makes text readable, pleasant, beautiful. This article Typography and Why it is Important for Your Website maintains that font selection gives your site its character. Looking at the examples below, you may feel it without reading the content: the first site is oficial, formal, it has feeling of power. The second site is fun and playful.

Sites with different "characters" The New York Times and Audio stories for kids

The example below shows the same site but with different typography. The first version of my site had simple fonts, in the second one I used fancy fonts, I think that the site on the right is more unique and more stylish.

The same site with different font types

Elements of Good Typography

In the article What is Typography I've read about the elements of good typography: consistency, hierarchy, alignment. The author of this article believes that these elements make the site look professional and keeps your readers focused on the content. I think these sites below are the good examples of professional typography. The first one shows large typeface combined with simple typefaces. I think it is a good design for cat web site. The second example shows Scientific American web site and its typeface helps to provide a scientific content.

The examples of good typography: Cat blog and Scientific American

As you can see typography helps to create content. It creates text, and it also creates "message" of a text and message of an image, if text is combined with image. You can also place image inside a text, it also makes a page stylish and beautiful. So text is both content and graphic element, and combining text and images makes both more powerful. The article The future of typography in Web design mentions this trend as superimposing text over images. The following sites are also the examples of decorative type font.

Superimposing text over images: Travel blog and Frank Digital

These sites are the great examples of hand drawn type. One of them is site for kids, so these stylish headings look great! Bright typography in combination with bright colors makes the site joyful and exciting. The other example shows stylish hand drawing heading in combination with clear design, so this site looks very professional.

Hand drawn style: Tate kids and XHTML Cafe

Fancy Fonts

You can find new possibilities to express your content with fancy fonts. For example, I've developed web site for kids as my project for web development class. In this site I used fancy font for quotes from the different kids movies. I think it was a good idea to stand apart the quotes from the other content and to highlight direct speech (example on left). I also used fancy fonts for header of my site (example on right).

Examples of using fancy fonts

Fancy fonts are embedded to web page using @font-face style. You have to download your chosen font and to add link via the font-family property. There is an option without downloading the font. You can use Google Font API: just find the font you like from the gallery, copy the embed link and add it to the head of your HTML document. Then you can access the font via the font-family property in your style sheet. I used this article The 10 Best Script and Handwritten Google Web Fontsfor fonts for this page. Both options work good if you want to add unique font to your design. When using fancy fonts, remember about readability: some fonts could be stylish but hard to read. The other problem is supporting a font by all browsers.
