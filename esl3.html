Multiple and Emotional Intelligences

The authors (David Miller Sadker and Myra Pollack Sadker) emphases that our understanding of intelligence has been transformed in the last 20-30 years because of the works of Gardner in multiple intelligences and Goleman in emotional intelligence. It turned out that intelligence covers not only logic and math problem solving but also social skills and various of abilities. That is why we should change our understanding of what schools are about. I like the author's phrase that we should "increase the range and diversity of educational ideas". I'll try to figure out what do these words mean.

What is intelligence

Gardner identified 8 kinds of intelligence. He mentioned that meaning of "intelligence" varies in different cultures. In the Pacific Islands it means navigation and orientation in space while for many Muslims it is the ability to memorize the Koran. Physical grace is the mark of intelligence for Balinese. The aim of his research is to explain why "the quality of an individual's performance may vary greatly in different activities" and how to change educational ideas to respond to different kinds of intelligence.

How multiple intelligences approach should change schools

According to the authors, teachers may do the following to change instructions and assessments:

  • use music to emphasize key points
  • promote hand and bodily movements to enhance learning
  • incorporate sharing and interpersonal interaction
  • teach children to control their feelings and emotions
  • use visual aids to promote understanding
  • encourage students to classify and appreciate world around them
    •

The assessment methods should also be expanded because tests are not enough to evaluate a student. They suggested to broad estimation methods with tangible artifacts, visual things, team projects, student's portfolio, etc.

Emotional intelligence

"Emotional intelligence involves the ability to monitor one's own and others emotions, to discriminate among them, and to use the information to guide one's thinking and actions."

Kelly & Moon 1998.

Acording to Goleman, the main characteristics of emotional intelligence are:

  • knowing emotions that means knowing yourself
  • managing emotions that means controlling yourself
  • motivating oneself that enables confidence, concentration
  • recognizing emotions in others that means empathy
  • handling relationships.

I think that the main idea of this article is the following: we all excel in different ways, we are capable in different ways, and we learn in different ways. That is why teachers have to use a variety of instructions and assessments in order to "more accurately reflect the varying nature of human capability."
